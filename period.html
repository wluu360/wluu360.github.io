Time Period











The first Ichthyosaurs  appeared around 250 million years ago at the beginning of the Mesozoic Era. Ichthyosaur populations continued to grow late into the Triassic period, reaching their peak diversity in the Jurassic period.














Fact:

Largest Ichthyosaur found is the size of a Blue whale.







Extinction

According to the fossil record, the last Ichthyosaurs lived around 100 million years ago, between the mid to late Cretaceous Period. The exact cause of their extinction is still under investigation, however there are many plausible theories that may explain how these formerly diverse species entered extinction:

After these events, the surviving lineages of Ichthyosaurs still faced many environmental challenges to their survival, and went extinct shortly after.



RISE IN COMPETITION

Although the Ichthyosaurs were highly diversified in the Jurassic period, they were still considered relatively primitive marine animals. As a result, they were not able to quickly adapt to the rapidly changing marine environment during the Cretaceous period. A changing marine environment would have resulted in a rise in competition for resources. Due to their primitive nature, the Ichthyosaurs were not able to adapt to the rise in competition for food, and thus gone into extinction due to their inability to out compete the other marine predators.
